The Valley of the Kings

  The Valley of the Kings – one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world, and one of Egypt’s most spectacular destinations is best visited with an Egyptoligist, so you can learn and understand the historical background of what you’re seeing, You can also go on your own by taxi. Hire the taxi for the entire day so you can visit the Valley of the Kings and other sites along the west bank.

  Begin at the Visitor Center, where you will purchase your entrance ticket, photography passes, and entrance tickets for extra tombs. Tombs that are not included in your basic ticket include:

  • Ramesses V and VI
    • 100 EGP per person
  • Seti I
    • 1,000 EGP per person, and arguably the best tomb
  • Tutankhamun
    • 300 EGP per person
    • Although King Tut’s tomb is one of the smallest at the Valley of Kings, seeing the young king is a highlight.

Travel Tips

  Rules regarding photography are constantly changing. The most recent rules state that if you want to take pictures with equipment other than your smartphone, you must purchase a photopass. In Egypt, most things must be paid for in cash as credit cards are not widely accepted. Additionally, there are no ATMs at the east bank of the Valley of the Kings or the Visitor Center, so you should bring cash with you for Luxor.
