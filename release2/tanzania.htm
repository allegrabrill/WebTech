Tanzania - Know Before You Go

General Information

  Tanzania, the largest country in East Africa, includes the islands of Zanzibar, Pemba, and Mafia. Tanzania is about twice the size of California, and is bordered by the Indian Ocean and eight countries: Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Democratic Republic of Congo, Zambia, Malawi, and Mozambique.

  Tanzania’s landscapes are legendary, from the steep ridges of the Great Rift Valley and the vast Ngorongoro Crater, to the grasslands of the Serengeti and Mount Kilimanjaro. Mount Kilimanjaro is the highest point in Africa, bordered by three of the largest lakes on the continent: in the north, Lake Victoria (the world’s second-largest freshwater lake); in the west, Lake Tanganyika; and in the southwest, Lake Nyasa.

  Most of the land was once savanna and bush, but today it is semi-desert and home to an abundance of wildlife. The largest remaining elephant populations in the world are in Tanzania’s Selous Game Reserve boasts the world’s largest remaining elephant populations. Many of the African mammal species are native to Tanzania, including wildebeests, zebras, giraffes, rhinos, lions, and leopards. Most of them, including elephants, are endangered due to poaching.

  Some of Africa’s most famous national parks and natural attractions are in Tanzania. It’s no surprise that the most popular things to do are safaris and wildlife adventures.

Cuisine

  Ugali is the most popular and traditional Tanzanian dish, which goes great with banana beer. Ugali is a type of porridge similar to fufu that originates in East Africa. Ugali is traditionally eaten with meat or mashed vegetables.

Travel Tips

  Parts of Tanzania are infested with the tsetse fly, a blood-sucking insect that transmits sleeping sickness to humans and livestock. Malaria is also a threat in this country.
